This article is about the 1759 battle. For the 1693 battle, see Battle of Lagos (1693).

The naval Battle of Lagos took place between a British fleet commanded by Sir Edward Boscawen and a French fleet under Jean-François de La Clue-Sabran
over two days in 1759 during the Seven Years' War . They fought south west of the Gulf of Cádiz on 18 August and to the east of the small Portuguese port of Lagos , which the battle is named, on 19 August.
